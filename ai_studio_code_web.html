About Me

I am a Research Scientist at the Center for Hydrologic Innovations at Arizona State University (ASU). My research focuses on understanding hydrological processes in both natural and urban environments and their interactions with ecological and climatic systems.

I specialize in combining ground observations, remote sensing datasets (from traditional satellites and CubeSat constellations), and regional hydrologic models to advance water sustainability and resilience. My recent work involves high-performance computing applications for modeling dryland hydrologic changes and flash flood hazards.

Research Interests

  • Hydrologic Modeling: Regional and distributed modeling of watershed systems.
  • Remote Sensing: Utilizing CubeSats and satellite data for streamflow detection and flood mapping.
  • Urban Ecohydrology: Analyzing irrigation cooling capacities and water conservation in urban settings.
  • Water Sustainability: Climate change impacts on water resources, specifically in the Colorado River Basin.
